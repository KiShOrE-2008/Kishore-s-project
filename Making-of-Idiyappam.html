Making of Idiyappam

Idiyappam, also known as string hoppers, is a traditional South Indian and Sri Lankan dish made from rice flour. It is a popular breakfast item and is often served with coconut milk or curry.


Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice flour (preferably roasted or idiyappam flour)
  • 1.25 to 1.3 cups hot water
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon oil or ghee (optional, for added softness)
  • Freshly grated coconut (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Roast the Rice Flour (Optional but Recommended):
      2. In a pan, lightly roast the rice flour on low heat for about 3–4 minutes until it becomes warm and slightly aromatic. This step helps in achieving softer idiyappam.
  2. Prepare the Dough:
    1. In a mixing bowl, add the roasted rice flour and salt.
    2. In a separate saucepan, bring 1.25 to 1.3 cups of water to a boil.
    3. Gradually pour the hot water into the rice flour, stirring continuously to prevent lumps.
    4. Once the mixture starts to come together, cover the bowl and let it sit for 5 minutes.
    5. After resting, add oil or ghee and knead the mixture into a smooth, non-sticky dough.
  3. Shape the Idiyappam:
    1. Grease the idiyappam press or sevai maker with oil or ghee.
    2. Divide the dough into portions and fill the press.
    3. Press the dough onto idli plates in concentric circles to form round discs
  4. Steam the Idiyappam:
    1. Steam the pressed idiyappam in an idli steamer or a regular steamer for 8–10 minutes.
    2. Once done, remove the plates and let them cool slightly before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Idiyappam can be served with:

  • Sweetened coconut milk (made with coconut milk, jaggery, and cardamom)
  • Spicy curries (like chicken curry, vegetable curry, or fish curry)
  • Grated coconut mixed with sugar for a simple sweet version
